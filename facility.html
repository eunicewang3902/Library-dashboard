Visual insights for maintaining optimal temperature and reducing noise in library spaces.

Keys

PMV (Predicted Mean Vote): A thermal comfort index predicting how people perceive temperature, ranging from cold (-3) to hot (+3), with 0 indicating optimal comfort.

NC (Noise Criterion): A measure of background noise level in a space — lower NC ratings mean quieter, more comfortable acoustic environments.

The relationship between Temperature & PMV, and Noise Level & NC Rating.

The Graphs Correlations

PMV and temperature have a positive correlation — as air temperature increases, PMV values rise, meaning people feel warmer; as temperature decreases, PMV values drop, and people feel cooler. The most comfortable PMV range is around 0, which typically occurs at 21–23°C under normal clothing and activity levels.

The NC (Noise Criterion) rating and noise level (dB) are positively correlated — as noise levels in decibels (dB) increase, the NC rating also increases. A higher NC rating means a noisier environment, while a lower NC rating (typically below 40) indicates a quieter and more comfortable space for activities like studying.
